Sim pedals - Part one

This 3d model of sim pedals was made in Onshape, a browers based CAD program with possibilities to collaborate with others in real time. To run this program you do not need a very powerfull pc, because onshape handles most work on their side. Switching to Onshape from another cad software is very doable within a few hours. It already showed to be usefull during module 3 when thanks to Onshape the entire project group was able to show and edit the 3D model of the projects casing.

Sim pedals - Part two

Part two of the sim pedals is the building of the actual pedals and combining it with the elctronics. The electronics consist of an arduino pro micro, which has an ATmega32u4 chip that allows it to be recognized as a (game)controller, and 3 potentiometers (one for each pedal). When pressing the pedal the potentiometer turns and the arduino communicates these values to the computer. The pedals themselves are 3d printed based on the (edited) model from part one. The spring in the pedals is also 3D printed, the only non-printed parts are the bolts holding everything together. The pedals are made out of multiple smaller parts to be able to prototype fast and replace parts if necessary. Link to the online and live updated 3D model: Click Here.

3D printer

This 3D printer is self build, it is connected to a raspberry pi and is therefore accessible through the internet. The base of the printer is a cr-10s frame with dual z-screws. The powersupply and heated bed are also from a cr-10s. The printhead is printed with another printer and the motherboard was replaced with a 32 bit version. I changed the firmware to make it work with the new motherboard and the self build printer. When everything was working I added a raspberry pi with Octoprint to control the printer remotely.

AI cooking assistant

This AI cooking assistant is powered by AiCall's AI technology and interacts with you trough speech to text and text to speech. The product (named Gordon) is a 3D printed housing with a raspberry pi inside, it runs an adjusted version of AiCall's code and is triggered by a motion sensor on the bottom. The user can ask Gordon all sort of kitchen related questions, such as: "Hey, Gordon. Can you provide me 3 pasta recipes, keep in mind that they have to be vegetarian." or "Hey, Gordon. How should I use my Miele oven and what should the temperature be according to the recipe?"
